MAY 18

Poetry Reading & Open Mic Night

📅 May 18, 2024 - 7:30 PM | 📍 Poetry Corner

Experience the power of spoken word at our monthly poetry reading and open mic night. Featured poets will share their original work, followed by an open mic session where audience members can share their own poems or favorite verses.

This event celebrates the diversity of voices in our community and provides a supportive space for both established and emerging poets. We'll explore various styles and themes, from personal reflections to social commentary.

Whether you're a seasoned poet or someone who simply enjoys the rhythm of language, this event offers something for everyone. Our host will provide tips for those interested in performing, and we'll have poetry collections available for inspiration.

Light refreshments and a welcoming atmosphere make this a perfect evening for literary enthusiasts. Come prepared to be moved, inspired, and perhaps even share a piece of your own.

MAY 25

Local History Lecture: The City's Literary Heritage

📅 May 25, 2024 - 6:30 PM | 📍 Lecture Hall

Join local historian Dr. Robert Kim for an fascinating exploration of our city's rich literary heritage. Discover the writers, poets, and literary movements that have shaped our cultural landscape over the past two centuries.

Dr. Kim will share stories of famous authors who called our city home, hidden literary landmarks, and the role of local publishing houses in shaping American literature. This illustrated presentation will include rare photographs and documents.

Learn about the literary salons that once flourished in our city, the influence of immigrant communities on our literary traditions, and contemporary writers who continue to draw inspiration from our unique history.

This event is free and open to all. Books related to local history will be available for browsing, and Dr. Kim will be available for questions following the presentation. A reception with light refreshments will follow.
